Skin Care Products

Citral is a component of lemongrass oil and the oils of verbena, lemon myrtle, lemon and orange. In cosmetics and personal care products, Citral is used in the formulation of many types of products including aftershave lotions, bath products, moisturizers, perfumes and colognes, skin care products and suntan products.

Citral can be synthesized from Isoprene.

Budavari, S.The Merck Index-Encyclopedia of Chemicals, Drugs and Biologicals. Rahway, NJ: Merck and Co., Inc., 1989.
Citral Essential Oil

Citral Essential Oil has a strong, lemony and herbaceous scent. It is a thin, clear, pale yellow liquid. Citral has been used in treatments of Acne, athlete's foot, excessive perspiration, flatulence, insect repellent, muscle aches, oily skin, scabies, and stress. Traditional Indian medicine has used Citral Essential Oil to treat infectious illness and fever.

Citral is principal constituent of lemongrass oil and can be isolated by fractional distillation. Obtained synthetically by oxidation of geraniol, nerol, or linalool by chromic acid.

Toxic

Citral can be toxic if it swallowed.

Flammable

Citral can ignite and continue to burn in air if exposed to a source of ignition. Aggregated GHS information provided by 248 companies from 4 notifications to the ECHA C&L Inventory is H225 (60.48%) or Highly Flammable liquid and vapor [Danger Flammable liquids], H226 (30.65%): Flammable liquid and vapor [Warning Flammable liquids].
If material on fire or involved in fire: Use foam, dry chemical, or carbon dioxide. Cool all affected containers with flooding quantities of water. Apply water from as far a distance as possible. Keep run-off water out of sewers and water sources.

Irritant

Citral is a local irritant. It can making your skin corrosion. When heated to decomposition it emits acrid smoke and fumes. CItral also can sauses a serius eye irritation or serious eye damage. Aggregated GHS information provided by 248 companies from 4 notifications to the ECHA C&L Inventory is H315 (100%): Causes skin irritation, H319 (60.48%): Causes serious eye irritation.
If exposed to skin rinse and then wash skin with water and soap and if exposed to eye rinse with plenty of water (remove contact lenses if easily possible).

Allergic

Citral may cause allergic for some people. Aggregated GHS information provided by 248 companies from 4 notifications to the ECHA C&L Inventory is H317 (30.65%): May cause an allergic skin reaction.

Hazardous for environment

Citral is hazardous to the aquatic environment. It is a long-term hazard. Aggregated GHS information provided by 248 companies from 4 notifications to the ECHA C&L Inventory is H412 (91.13%): Harmful to aquatic life with long lasting effects.
